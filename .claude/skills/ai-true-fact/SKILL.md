# AI true fact

Use this skill to generate one or more facts about artificial intelligence that are intended to be true.

## Behavior

1. Prefer widely accepted, non-controversial facts over niche claims.
2. Do not invent numbers, dates, citations, model capabilities, or historical details.
3. If the request is broad, provide general AI facts.
4. If the request names a topic such as training, inference, machine learning, LLMs, robotics, or AI history, keep the facts scoped to that topic.
5. If a claim seems uncertain, replace it with a safer and better-established fact.

## Output

- Return concise bullet points.
- Each bullet should contain one fact.
- Keep the wording direct and plain.
- Do not add made-up sources or fake references.

## Example

- Machine learning is a subset of AI focused on systems that improve performance from data.
- Neural networks are inspired by biological neurons, but they are simplified mathematical models rather than literal digital brains.
